Soft Wash vs. Pressure Wash in Broward: What to Use (and Where)

Updated: 2025-08-18 · 7–9 min read

Both methods clean exterior surfaces, but they aren’t interchangeable. In Broward County, soft washing protects roofs and delicate finishes, while pressure cleaning restores hard surfaces like driveways, sidewalks, and pavers.

Quick Comparison

MethodBest ForNot For
Soft Wash (low pressure)Roofs, stucco, painted siding, screensDeep oil stains in concrete
Pressure CleaningConcrete, pavers, curbs, dumpster padsFragile coatings or aging shingles

Where Soft Wash Shines

For roof cleaning and delicate surfaces like stucco and painted trim, soft washing lifts organic growth with minimal risk. It’s the right choice for HOA compliance and long-term maintenance.

Where Pressure Cleaning Wins

Use pressure cleaning on hard surfaces that can handle it—driveways, sidewalks, and pavers. Surface cleaners deliver even results and remove algae, gum, and tire marks.

Combining Methods for the Best Results

Most Broward properties benefit from a combined approach: soft wash for roof/house, pressure clean for ground-level surfaces. It’s efficient, consistent, and photo-friendly for listings and board reports.
